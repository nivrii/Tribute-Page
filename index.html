Professor Wangari Maathai

The woman who loved trees

The biography of Wangari Maathai

  • 1940

    She was born on in Nyeri, Kenya

  • 1971

    She became the first woman in East and Central Africa to obtain a Ph.D. from the University of Nairobi.

  • 1977

    She founded the Green Belt Movement, an environmental NGO that encourages women to plant trees

  • 1984

    She won the Right Livelihood Award for her environmental activism

  • 2002

    She was elected to the Parliament of Kenya with 98 percent of the vote

  • 2004

    She became the first African woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize for her contribution to sustainable development, democracy, and peace

  • 2011

    She died of ovarian cancer in Nairobi, Kenya
